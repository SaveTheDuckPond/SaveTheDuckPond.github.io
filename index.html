What is the Ridgewood Wild Duck Pond?

The Ridgewood Duck Pond is a public Bergen County park. It can be accessed from the south using the path which runs along a 2 mile stretch that wraps around the river or by car from the entrance on East Ridgewood Avenue. The main attractions of the park include picnic areas, a playground and a dog park, activities include fishing, ice skating when weather conditions allow, and of course... the pond. The Pond is dying, however. As wells drain the surrounding groundwater, and little to no water conming in, the pond is getting drier and drier.

There are three main reasons that the duck pond is dying/getting worse:

Tilted Weir

The duck pond's source of water comes from a nearby river/brook commonly known as the Saddle River. A weir is basically a barrier in that river that changes the flow of the water from it's natural state. In the case of the Duck Pond, the barrier is sort of a cinder-blockish thing that diverts water from the river into the pond.
